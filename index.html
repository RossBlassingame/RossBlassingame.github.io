about me


In Summer 2017, I had an internship with Charles Schwab on the Compliance and Legal Services team. A year later I graduated and joined the same team, and did some interesting work on various Schwab-internal apps. One of my favorite projects on that team was rewriting a legacy app, originally built using .NET Framework, in newer technologies such as Angular 2+ and .NET Core 2.1. I also built out the whole CI/CD pipeline, which used Bamboo to deploy it to our cloud environment, PCF.


On my current team, the Intelligent Portfolios team, most of my time at the moment is spent working in the UI using Angular 7-9, or working on the backend using .NET Core 2.1 (or the .NET Framework for our more legacy stuff). I really enjoy UI/UX work; one of my favorite courses in college was an accessibility course, which taught me how to design software accessible to all people.


When I'm not working, you can sometimes find me reading. Currently, I'm reading "How to be an Anti-Racist", by Ibram X. Kendi. My last book was "The Great Alone", by Kristin Hannah. If you're looking for a recommendation, you can't go wrong with either - they're both excellent. Here are some more books I've read recently:

  • Why I'm No Longer Talking to White People About Race
  • Why How Not to Die: Discover the Foods Scientifically Proven to Prevent and Reverse Disease


Another hobby of mine is running. Since the pandemic started, I've been running 25-30 mpw as an outlet. I was planning on doing the 2020 BolderBoulder and Colfax Marathon, but unfortunately both were cancelled.
